The Discovery Problem

Most authors don't have a quality problem. They have a visibility problem. Here's how to tell the difference.

Why Good Books Stay Invisible

Every day, thousands of well-written, beautifully edited, professionally covered books launch into the void. Their authors have done everything "right" — yet months later, they're wondering why nobody's buying.

The answer isn't that readers don't want their books. The answer is that readers literally cannot find them. Amazon has over 15 million books. In that ocean, your book is a single drop — unless you build the infrastructure that makes it visible.

The 8 Signs Your Book Has a Discovery Problem

If even 2–3 of these feel familiar, your book almost certainly has a positioning gap.

📉

"My reviews are great but nobody's finding the book"

You have a 4.0+ rating. Readers who find it love it. But your BSR is stuck past #500,000 and organic discovery has stalled. The quality is there. The visibility infrastructure is not.

🔇

"I launched and it just... disappeared"

You did the launch posts, told your friends, maybe ran a small promo. There was a brief spike, then nothing. That's a launch strategy gap, not a book problem.

🎯

"I don't know who my readers are"

You wrote the book, but you can't describe your ideal reader with precision. Without that map, marketing is just guessing.

📂

"I think I'm in the wrong categories"

Amazon has 16,000+ categories. Being in the wrong 3 means your book is invisible to readers who would love it.

📖

"Readers love Book 1 but never buy Book 2"

Your series has a read-through leak. The cause is almost always a back-matter funnel gap.

👻

"My backlist is just sitting there"

Those books still have value — they just need repositioning and reintroduction to communities that have grown.

😰

"Marketing feels overwhelming"

Nobody told you which tactics actually matter for YOUR book in YOUR genre. You need a filter, not more noise.

🤷

"I'm spending money but can't tell if it's working"

You have no framework to evaluate what moved the needle and what was wasted money.
