Sound familiar?

The 8 signs your book has a discovery problem, not a quality problem

If even 2-3 of these feel familiar, your book almost certainly has a positioning gap that's blocking readers from finding it.

📉

"My reviews are great but nobody's finding the book"

You have a 4.0+ rating. Readers who find it love it. But your BSR is stuck past #500,000 and organic discovery has completely stalled. The quality is there. The visibility infrastructure is not.

🔇

"I launched and it just... disappeared"

You did the launch posts, told your friends, maybe ran a small promo. There was a brief spike, then nothing. Three months later, it feels like the book never existed. That's a launch strategy gap, not a book problem.

🎯

"I don't know who my readers are or where they gather"

You wrote the book, but you can't describe your ideal reader with precision. You don't know which categories they browse, which comp titles they love, or which communities they belong to. Without that map, marketing is just guessing.

📂

"I think I'm in the wrong categories but I don't know which ones are right"

Amazon has 16,000+ categories. Being in the wrong 3 means your book is invisible to readers who would love it. This is the single most common fixable problem, and most authors don't even know it's happening.

📖

"Readers love Book 1 but never buy Book 2"

Your series has a read-through leak. Readers finish Book 1 and vanish. The cause is almost always a back-matter funnel gap: no next-book link, no newsletter CTA, or a Book 2 blurb that doesn't hook the reader who just finished Book 1.

👻

"I have a backlist that's just sitting there doing nothing"

You published 2, 3, or 5+ books. The early ones have stopped selling entirely. But those books still have value. They just need repositioning, refreshed metadata, and reintroduction to communities that have grown since they launched.

😰

"Marketing feels overwhelming and I don't know what actually works"

You've been told to do newsletters, BookTok, Instagram, blog tours, Amazon ads, Facebook groups, Goodreads giveaways, and 47 other things. Nobody told you which of those actually matter for YOUR book in YOUR genre. You need a filter, not more noise.

🤷

"I'm spending money on marketing but can't tell if it's working"

You've tried ads, promos, or paid services. Maybe something helped, maybe not. You have no framework to evaluate what moved the needle and what was wasted money. You need a data baseline, not more tactics.
